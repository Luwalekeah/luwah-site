# Local SEO Without a Public Home Address

You run Luwah Technologies from home and serve the entire USA. You can still get
local-pack visibility and Google reviews without showing your home address. Here
is the playbook for when you have time.

## The core move: a service-area business profile

1. Create a **Google Business Profile** at business.google.com.
2. During setup, when asked for an address, choose **"I deliver goods and
   services to my customers"** and turn **off** "I also serve customers at my
   business address." This makes it a service-area business (SAB).
3. Enter your home address only for **verification**. Google keeps it hidden.
   The public profile shows your service areas, not your street.
4. Set service areas to the Denver metro cities you serve (Denver, Aurora,
   Lakewood, Centennial, etc.). Service areas are regional, so this is your
   local footprint. National reach comes from organic content, not the profile.
5. Verify by postcard, phone, or video (Google picks the method).

Once live, the SAB profile still collects **Google reviews** through your review
link, so you get review credibility without exposing your house.

## Two tracks, kept separate

- **Local (Denver):** the GBP profile above plus local citations. This wins the
  Denver map pack.
- **National (whole USA):** organic content and authority. The weekly blog, the
  case studies (Doyle Group, Solephi in Florida, Kany), and on-page SEO. GBP
  does not drive national reach, so do not expect it to.

## Reviews and rich results

- Get reviews on the GBP profile (Denver/local signal) and on third-party B2B
  platforms (Clutch, G2, Trustpilot). Those platforms are where star rich
  results in search come from.
- The on-site review section we built is for trust and conversion. Do **not**
  add self-serving `Review` or `AggregateRating` structured data to your own
  Organization markup. Google disallows it and it can trigger a manual penalty.
  Show the stars visually, keep the schema markup off them.

## NAP consistency

- Keep Name and Phone identical everywhere: site, GBP, directories, social.
- No public address is needed. Use city + "service area: Denver metro, serving
  the USA" as your location language.

## Citations and directories to claim

- Google Business Profile (SAB)
- Bing Places (also supports SAB)
- Apple Business Connect
- Clutch and G2 (B2B tech services)
- Local Denver chambers or small-business directories

## Checklist

1. Create GBP as a service-area business, address hidden.
2. Set Denver-metro service areas.
3. Verify.
4. Add categories (e.g. "Software company", "Business management consultant"),
   hours, services, and photos.
5. Send your GBP review link to happy clients.
6. Claim Bing Places and Apple Business Connect.
7. Build out Clutch and G2 profiles for national B2B credibility.
